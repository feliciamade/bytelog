Computer Vision is a subset of Artificial Intelligence that enables computers to see and interpret images and videos. Computer vision excels when used for special effects in movies, augmented reality, intelligent transportation systems, and more. However, computer vision is not always reliable. An example of computer vision being unreliable is when it was used in a criminal case to determine the suspect of a theft crime. The individual was falsey acussed and had to spend 30 hours in jail. To combat this issue, researches and companies are actively improving facial recogintion so it can be fairer and more accurate. In addition, they are builder smarter algorithums that focus on the right features and treat everyone equally. In addition, they are learning how to interpret tricky photos by sharpening the images, fixing angles and lightening, liveness checks, and more.
